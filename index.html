Mechanical Design & Structure

Frame and Chassis: Must be strong enough to support all components and withstand field conditions. Lightweight yet rigid frames reduce energy consumption.

Moving Parts: Seed metering mechanism, furrow openers, seed delivery system, and wheels. Proper alignment and tolerances prevent clogging or uneven planting.

Power Transmission: Gear trains, belts, chains, or motors that drive seed metering and movement. Efficiency and reliability here are crucial.

Seed Metering & Planting Accuracy

Seed Singulation: Ensures seeds are spaced evenly to optimize crop yield.

Depth Control: Accurate depth planting helps uniform germination.

Seed Type Compatibility: The machine should handle the seed sizes you intend to plant.

Mechanism Type: Could be plate, fluted wheel, vacuum, or vibration-assisted dispensing.

Control & Operational Efficiency

Automation vs Manual: How much of the planting process is automated? Manual machines rely on operator skill, automated ones need sensors or motors.

Speed & Capacity:Planting rate should match field size and time constraints.

Adjustability & Safety: Adjustable settings for different seeds or soil types, and safety mechanisms to prevent jamming or damage.
